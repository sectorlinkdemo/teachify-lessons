Modal Verbs
A2/B1 Grammar Clinic 6 Exercise Types
Can, could, should, must, might — expressing ability, obligation, advice, and possibility.
Score 0 / 0 correct
📖 The Rule
ModalMain usesExample
canAbility (present) / permissionI can speak three languages.
couldAbility (past) / polite request / possibilityCould you send me the file? / She could be in a meeting.
shouldAdvice / recommendation / mild obligationYou should speak to your manager.
mustStrong obligation / logical deductionYou must complete the form. / He must be tired.
might / mayPossibility (weaker than must)She might be late. / It may rain.
have toExternal obligation (rule/requirement)I have to submit it by Friday.
don't have toNo obligation (not necessary)You don't have to attend. (but you can)
mustn'tProhibition (not allowed)You mustn't share the password.
⚠️ Key difference: Don't have to = not necessary. Mustn't = forbidden. These are completely different!
You don't have to wear a tie. (optional)   vs   You mustn't be late. (prohibited)
⚠️ Common Mistakes
✗ You must to finish the report today.
✓ You must finish the report today. (no 'to' after modals)
✗ She can to speak English very well.
✓ She can speak English very well.
✗ I don't must work on Sundays.
✓ I don't have to work on Sundays.
✗ He should to call the client.
✓ He should call the client.
