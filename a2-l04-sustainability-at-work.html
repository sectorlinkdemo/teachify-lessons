Article
Can Your Company Go Green?

More and more companies are trying to reduce their impact on the environment. Sustainability is no longer just a trend — it is becoming a business priority.

One of the biggest ways a company can reduce its carbon footprint is by changing how employees commute. Encouraging cycling, public transport, or remote work reduces emissions significantly.

Companies can also switch to renewable energy sources for their offices, reduce paper use, and choose sustainable suppliers. Some organisations set targets to become carbon neutral by a certain year.

Sustainability policies also attract talent. Many younger employees want to work for companies that share their environmental values. Going green is not just good for the planet — it can be good for business too.

Read & Discuss
Short Dialogue
A:
Have you seen the new sustainability policy the company published?
B:
Yes, briefly. They want to reduce our carbon footprint by 40% by 2030.
A:
That's ambitious. How are they planning to do it?
B:
They're switching to renewable energy and encouraging staff to change how they commute.
A:
I already cycle to work, so I'm doing my part. But what about business travel?
B:
They're reducing flights and asking people to use video calls instead.
A:
Good. Flights have a huge impact on emissions. Every little reduction helps.
B:
Exactly. And apparently clients are asking about our sustainability record now too.

Comprehension

  1. What is the company's target for 2030?
  2. What two changes are the company making?
  3. Why does A think reducing flights is important?
